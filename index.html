Which video best performs the described motion?

A short, anonymous study. No login, no personal data. Takes about 10–15 minutes.
  1. For each scenario you'll see a short text instruction (the intended edit) and several short video clips.
  2. Pick your top 3 clips — the ones that best perform the described motion while looking natural. Click clips in order: 1st click = 1 2nd click = 2 3rd click = 3. Click a chosen clip again to undo it.
  3. For each of your top 3, set two quick ratings (0–5), where red = poor and green = excellent:
    • Motion quality — does the motion happen smoothly and naturally? Sudden jumps, jitter, flickering, or unnatural/physically-impossible movement = poor.
    • Visual quality — does the image stay clean and consistent? Identity collapse, subjects changing, shifts in appearance or colors, blur, or artifacts = poor.
  4. Finally, mark the single worst clip with a red ✗ (no rating needed).
  5. There are 8 scenarios. A clip looping = it just repeats; watch as long as you like.
No names or emails are collected.
